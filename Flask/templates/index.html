About

South Indian dance, also known as "Dravidian" dance, encompasses a rich and diverse array of classical and folk dance forms originating from the southern region of India. These dance forms are deeply rooted in ancient Indian culture, mythology, and religious traditions, and they often convey stories, emotions, and spiritual themes through graceful movements, intricate footwork, and expressive gestures.

Indian dance classification refers to the categorization and study of the diverse array of traditional dance forms originating from the Indian subcontinent. These dance forms are deeply rooted in Indian culture, history, mythology, and religious traditions, and they exhibit a wide range of styles, techniques, and regional variations.

Types of Dance Forms

Bharatnatyam


  • Bharatanatyam is one of the oldest and most widely practiced classical dance forms of India, originating from the state of Tamil Nadu.

  • Bharatanatyam is traditionally performed by female dancers and is characterized by its emphasis on storytelling, mythology, and spirituality.

  • Dancers undergo rigorous training to master the technique, including learning adavus (basic steps), abhinaya (expression), and talas (rhythmic patterns)..

  • The dance is accompanied by Carnatic music, with compositions in Sanskrit, Tamil, Telugu, and other languages.

  • Bharatanatyam costumes are elaborate and typically include a colorful silk saree, adorned with jewelry and accessories like anklets, earrings, and hair ornaments.

Kathak


  • Kathak is one of the eight classical dance forms of India, originating from North India.

  • Kathak is traditionally performed in temples, courts, and royal palaces, and it often depicts stories from Hindu mythology and epics.

  • Kathak dancers often perform intricate rhythmic patterns (tukras, tihais) and fast spins (chakkars) accompanied by tabla and other classical music instruments.

  • Costumes for Kathak performances typically include elaborate traditional attire such as ghagra-choli for females and kurta-dhoti for males.

  • Today, Kathak continues to thrive as both a classical art form and a popular form of entertainment, with performances held in India and around the world.

Kuchupudi


  • Kuchipudi is a classical Indian dance form that originated in the village of Kuchipudi in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh.

  • Kuchipudi incorporates elements of Natya (drama), Nritta (pure dance), and Nritya (expressive dance).

  • Dancers wear traditional costumes including brightly colored sarees for women and dhotis for men, adorned with jewelry and accessories.

  • The repertoire of Kuchipudi includes a wide range of themes, including mythological stories, devotional themes, and social issues.

  • Kuchipudi has gained international recognition and is performed on stages worldwide, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of India.

Kathakali


  • Kathakali is a traditional dance-drama form originating from the Indian state of Kerala.

  • Kathakali performances often depict stories from Hindu mythology, such as the Ramayana and Mahabharata.

  • Traditional Kathakali makeup, called "chutti," involves intricate designs and colors to represent different character.

  • The accompanying music, known as "sopanam," is typically performed using traditional percussion instruments and vocals.

  • Kathakali is recognized as one of the major forms of classical Indian dance and is revered for its cultural significance and artistic excellence.

Manipuri


  • Manipuri dance is a classical dance form originating from the northeastern state of Manipur in India.

  • Manipuri dance is deeply rooted in the rich cultural and religious traditions of Manipur, particularly the Vaishnavism and Shaktism traditions.

  • The dance form is characterized by its soft, flowing movements, which often depict themes from Hindu mythology, legends, and folklore.

  • Costumes in Manipuri dance are vibrant and elaborate, often featuring intricate embroidery, mirror work, and traditional motifs.

  • Manipuri dance has gained recognition and popularity worldwide for its aesthetic appeal, cultural significance, and spiritual depth.

Kathakali


  • Odissi is a classical dance form originating from the state of Odisha in Eastern India.

  • Odissi is known for its graceful movements, intricate footwork, and expressive gestures, known as mudras.

  • Odissi dancers often perform to traditional Odissi music, which includes compositions in Odia language and classical ragas.

  • Costumes and jewelry play a significant role in Odissi performances, with dancers wearing elaborate attire and ornaments.

  • Odissi has undergone revival and reconstruction efforts in the 20th century, leading to its recognition as one of the eight classical dance forms of India.

Sattriya


  • Sattriya is one of the eight classical dance forms of India, originating from the state of Assam

  • Sattriya dance was revived and codified as a classical dance form in the 20th century by Srimanta Sankardeva and his disciple Madhavdeva.

  • Sattriya is characterized by its graceful movements, intricate footwork, and expressive gestures, known as hastas.

  • Costumes in Sattriya dance often feature traditional Assamese attire, with elaborate headgear, silk sarees, and jewelry.

  • Sattriya dance has been recognized as an important cultural heritage of India and has gained popularity both within and outside Assam.

Mohiniyattam


  • Mohiniyattam is a classical dance form originating from Kerala, a state in South India.

  • Traditionally performed by female dancers, Mohiniyattam is known for its feminine elegance and charm.

  • Mohiniyattam often explores themes of love, devotion, and mythology, drawing inspiration from Hindu epics and legends.

  • Costumes in Mohiniyattam typically feature white or off-white sarees with gold borders, adorned with traditional jewelry and hair accessories.

  • Mohiniyattam is an integral part of Kerala's cultural heritage and is performed at festivals, temples, and cultural events.
